I intend to learn about deep learning. Which articles should I start reading?
------------------
If you are new to deep learning, here are some articles that can help you get started:

1. "Deep Learning" by Ian Goodfellow, Yoshua Bengio, and Aaron Courville.
   This book is freely available online and provides a comprehensive introduction to deep learning concepts and techniques.

2. "A Gentle Introduction to Deep Learning" by Sebastian Raschka.
   This article gives an overview of deep learning, its history, and the core components involved, making it an excellent starting point.

3. "Deep Learning: An Introduction for Applied Students" by Alex Smola.
   This article provides an introduction to deep learning, including its applications, key concepts, and implementation details, with a focus on practical aspects.

4. "The Unreasonable Effectiveness of Deep Learning" by Yann LeCun, Yoshua Bengio, and Geoffrey Hinton.
   This article discusses the advancements in deep learning and its impressive performance in various domains, highlighting its potential and impacts.

5. "Deep Learning Tutorial" by LISA lab, University of Montreal.
   This tutorial offers a detailed introduction to deep learning, covering topics like artificial neural networks, convolutional neural networks (CNNs), and recurrent neural networks (RNNs).

6. "Understanding Neural Networks and Deep Learning" by Michael Nielsen.
   This online book provides an in-depth understanding of neural networks and their relationship with deep learning, covering fundamental concepts and building intuition.

7. "Deep Learning 101" by Vincent Granville.
   This article serves as a high-level introduction to deep learning, giving an overview of its goals, applications, and key algorithms used.

Remember to refer to authoritative sources, such as academic papers or books, as you delve deeper into the subject. Additionally, hands-on experience through coding exercises or projects will greatly enhance your understanding and skills in deep learning.
